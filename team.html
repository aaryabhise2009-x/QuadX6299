Team History

2025 – 2026

This season was full of ambition and progress. With 6 rookies on the team, we primarily focused on curricula and our own QuadX Leadership Camp to promote learning. We built 2 fully functional robots capable of scoring from the far side with a consistent auto. We attended 7 company tours/visits including Tesla and Amazon, hosted STEM nights and Impulse camp, created team Viperia 32196, and fundraised $5,500 — with $1,300 going toward creating 2 teams in Rwanda in partnership with Stryke 6210.

Awards Won
FTC SWAT League Championships
3rd Inspire Award
2024 – 2025

Our 2024–2025 season had us taking on four new rookies. We focused on bringing them up to speed. While our robot game was not on par with the previous year, our outreach was great — including tours of Samsung and Dell facilities, large community events, and professional virtual meetings.

Awards Won
FTC Central WALL-E Championship
2nd Connect Award
2023 – 2024

For our 2023–2024 season, we only had two rookies on our team, with the remainder being veterans. Despite not advancing past regionals, we hosted events such as our coding basics camp CodeX and learned from several industry professionals.

Awards Won
FTC Central WALL-E Championship
3rd Inspire Award
FTC Central Area Championship
3rd Design Award
2022 – 2023 Worlds

The beginning of the season was rough due to little consideration for software in our design process. Thankfully, our team had much experience in judging, which helped us make it to Worlds and become a design award finalist. We also hosted a STEM event known as Momentum.

Awards Won
FTC Texas Central GEMS League Championships
3rd Inspire Award
FTC Texas Central Regional Championship
2nd Connect Award
FTC Texas State Championship
2nd Inspire Award
FTC World Championship – Franklin Division
3rd Design Award
2021 – 2022

With an almost entirely rookie team and the pandemic's aftermath, the 2021 season was difficult. Our end-of-year Momentum Career Showcase was held virtually, making STEM expertise accessible to more people. We continued building relationships with industry professionals and made big strides to increase commitment to robotics.

Awards Won
FTC Texas Central Austin Metro League Championship
1st Design Award
FTC Texas Central Championship

2020 – 2021

With an almost entirely rookie team and an emerging pandemic, the 2020 season was extremely difficult. Yet we persevered. The Argentinian camp we hosted was entirely in Spanish, making STEM resources more accessible. We continued our relationship with South Africa and further helped the Greater Tzaneen Community FTC teams.

A team of all veteran members, QuadX had a strong base for 2019–2020. We took on our biggest endeavor yet and travelled to Tzaneen, South Africa to set up 2 FTC teams. Our season was ultimately cut short due to COVID-19.
