Team History

2025-2026

This season was full of ambition and progress. With 6 rookies on the team, we primarily focused on cirriculums and our own QuadX Leadership Camp to promote learning. We built 2 fully funcional robot capable of scoring from the far side with a consistent auto. In terms of outreach, we attended 7 company tours/visits including places like Tesla and Amazon. We hosted many STEM nights and Impulse, a FIRST related 2 day summer camp. We created our own team Viperia 32196 and fundraises $5,500 this season, with $1,300 going in our efforts to create 2 teams in Rwanda in partnership with Stryke 6210 to buy 2 starter kits for the students in Rwanda.

Awards Won

FTC SWAT League Championships:
- Inspire 3'rd

2024-2025

Our 2024-2025 season had us taking on four different rookies. This year, we focused on having our new rookies brought up to speed, due to some of our notable veterans (namely our marketing lead) leaving at the end of the season. While our robot game was not on par with the previous year's robots, our outreach was great, with tours of Samsung and Dell facilities. Within this, there was also a large amount of community outreach (called Inertia) and professional virtual meetings. These learning experiences for the veterans and rookies was invaluable for improving next season's results.

Awards Won

FTC Central WALL-E Championship:
- Connect 2'nd

2023-2024

For our 2023-2024 season, we only had two rookies on our team, with the remainder being veterans. Despite this, we unfortunately were not able to advance past regionals. Even though our progression through the season was rough, we still took the time to outreach to our community by hosting events such as our coding basics camp CodeX, as well as learn several different techniques from industry professionals.

Awards Won

2022-2023

The beginning of the season was rough, due to little consideration for software in our design process, which caused problems early on. Thankfully, our team had much experience in judging, which helped us make it to worlds, as well as being a finalist for the design award in our division. Being at worlds was an incredible experience, with lots of excitement and fun to be had by the entire team. We also did a lot of outreach in multiple areas, even hosting a STEM event known as Momentum.

2021-2022

With an almost entirely rookie team and the pandemic's aftermath, the 2021 season was difficult but we also made big changes for the team as a whole. Meeting virtually was utilized heavily after the pandemic. Our end of year Momentum Career Showcase event was even held virtually, making STEM expertise accessible to more people. We continued to build our relationships with these industry professionals which influenced our future outreach opportunities. We also brought back some of our team traditions to boost team morale and made big strides to increase commitment to robotics. Despite the challenges we faced, we were able to influence and help many both in and outside of the STEM community while redefining what it means for us to be a successful team.

2020-2021

With an almost entirely rookie team and an emerging pandemic, the 2020 season was extremely difficult. Meeting times and spaces were restricted which made training our new team more challenging. Yet, we persevered and managed to reach many with FIRST values. The Argentinian camp we hosted was entirely in Spanish and available on our Youtube channel, making STEM resources and coding classes more accessible to those who do not speak English. We continued our relationship with South Africa and further helped the Greater Tzaneen Community FTC teams. We also continued our advocacy for STEM locally, speaking with legislature in hopes to improve STEM funding. Despite the many challenges of the pandemic, we were able to influence and help many.

2019-2020

A team of all veteran members, QuadX had a strong base for the 2019 - 2020 season. Wanting to push the boundaries of what a small group of individuals could achieve, we took on our biggest endeavor yet and travelled to Tzaneen, South Africa to set up 2 FTC teams. Coming so close to our goal of winning Inspire at Worlds last year we didn't want to leave anything on the table. Unfortunately, after an already successful season winning the Inspire awards at our League Championship and Regional Championship our season was cut short due to COVID-19.

WHAT IS FIRST

FIRST

For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology

FIRST is an acronym for "For The Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology". They are an international youth organization that run and administer the First Tech Challenge, First LEGO League Discover, FIRST Lego League Explore, First LEGO League Challenge and First Robotics Challenge robotics challenges. FIRST hosts these to promote and spread the idea of STEM (STEAM) by combining the excitement of sports and rigors of science and technology.

FIRST PROGRAMS

FIRST LEGO LEAGUE (FLL)

It is a program run by FIRST that helps introduce the values of STE(A)M to younger audiences (from K to 8) with the help of Lego products. The usage of them creates a fun game for the participants and supports learning about science and technology through the game itself and other aspects of the organization.

VISIT FLL WEBSITE

FIRST TECH CHALLENGE (FTC)

FTC allows students from grades 7-12 to participate in a robot competion that lasts for most of the school year. Students will learn skills of science and technology, while also developing important teamworking skills

VISIT FTC WEBSITE

FIRST ROBOTICS COMPETITION (FRC)

FRC allows high school students to participate in a robot competition that lasts for about half of the school year. Students will learn skills of science and technology, while also developing important teamworking skills.
