2024 – 2025

ATLAS

FTC: Into the Deep

Our most mechanically robust robot yet. ATLAS was built around a high-torque linear slide system and a reliable claw intake optimized for scoring specimens in the high chamber. Secured a regional Design Award for our engineering documentation.

2023 – 2024

MIDAS

FTC: CENTERSTAGE

Named for the golden touch — MIDAS featured a custom pixel-stacking arm and a reliable autonomous routine that scored consistently on the backdrop. A major leap forward in software autonomy for the team.

2022 – 2023

ANUBIS

FTC: POWERPLAY

Designed to dominate the junction stack — ANUBIS used a 4-bar linkage arm to precisely place cones at junction height. Won the Inspire Award at the league tournament for overall team performance and community impact.

2021 – 2022

HERCULES

FTC: FREIGHT FRENZY

Built in our team's comeback season after COVID. HERCULES featured a carousel mechanism for duck spinning and a side-mounted bucket intake for efficient freight scoring. A character-building season that re-established our competition presence.

2020 – 2021

N/A

FTC: ULTIMATE GOAL

Competed in Ultimate Goal, a long-distance shooting challenge where robots had to launch rings into goals at varying heights. A tough season that pushed the team's mechanical and programming capabilities.

2019 – 2020

HADES 2.0

FTC: SKYSTONE

An evolved rebuild of the original Hades platform. Added a stone-grabbing servo claw and improved autonomous pathing using odometry pods for the first time. The season was cut short in late March by the pandemic shutdown.

2018 – 2019

HELIOS

FTC: ROVER RUCKUS

HELIOS tackled the challenge of hanging from a lander and depositing minerals. Featured a scissor-lift hanging mechanism — our most complex end-game mechanism to that point — and a continuous servo intake for sampling minerals.

2017 – 2018

N/A

FTC: RELIC RECOVERY

Competed in Relic Recovery, tasked with scoring glyphs in the cryptobox and placing relics in designated zones. Laid the groundwork for the mechanical systems that would carry us through the next season.

2016 – 2017

APOLLO

FTC: VELOCITY VORTEX

Where it all started. APOLLO was our first robot — a simple flywheel shooter built to score balls into the center vortex. Rough around the edges, but the team that built it is the reason we're still here today.

2015 – 2016

HERMES 2.0

FTC: RES-Q

A revised iteration of the original HERMES built in our founding year. Competed in the RES-Q challenge with a passive climber and debris sweeper. The robot that taught the team what it means to iterate and improve under pressure.
